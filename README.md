# HR Analysis: Employee Attrition & Engagement Insights

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition, engagement, satisfaction, compensation, and demographic patterns within an organization. The goal is to identify key drivers of attrition and provide actionable HR recommendations to improve retention and workforce stability.

## 🎯 Business Objective
To understand the factors influencing employee attrition and engagement, and support data-driven HR decision-making around retention, performance, and workforce planning.

## 📊 Dataset Overview
The dataset contains employee-level data including:
- Job satisfaction and engagement metrics  
- Compensation and salary hikes  
- Demographics (age, gender, marital status, education)  
- Job roles and departments  
- Work patterns (overtime, business travel)  
- Promotion history  

## 🧱 Workforce Structure Summary
- Majority of employees fall within the **25–34 age group**  
- **R&D** is the largest department (961 employees), followed by Sales and HR  
- Most employees fall under **“Travel Rarely”**  

These distributions influence overall patterns and must be considered when interpreting results.

## 📌 Key Metrics
- Attrition Rate: 16%  
- Average Job Satisfaction: 2.7 / 4  
- Work-Life Balance: 2.7 / 4  
- Average Salary Hike: 15%  

While overall attrition appears moderate, deeper analysis reveals concentrated risk segments.

---

## 🔍 Attrition Analysis

### 💰 Salary Hike vs Attrition
- High salary hike attrition: 105 employees  
- Medium: 41 employees  
- Low: 91 employees  

High salary increases did not reduce attrition as expected.

**Interpretation:**
- Raises may be reactive (given to already dissatisfied employees)  
- Raises may come too late  
- High-performance roles may carry higher pressure  

---

### ⏳ Tenure & Promotion
- Employees with **0–5 years experience** had the highest attrition  
- Employees with **0–3 years since last promotion** left more  
- Employees with **11+ years since promotion** stayed the most  

**Insight:**
Early-stage employees are the most unstable, while long-tenured employees show strong retention regardless of promotion frequency.

---

### ⏱️ Overtime
- Employees with **no overtime** left more  

**Interpretation:**
- Overtime is not a driver of attrition  
- Employees without overtime may be less engaged or underutilized  

---

### ✈️ Business Travel
- “Travel Rarely” dominates both attrition and retention  

**Insight:**
This reflects workforce size, not a causal factor.

---

## 😊 Satisfaction & Engagement
- Lower satisfaction (job, environment, relationships) correlates with higher attrition  
- However, differences are moderate → attrition is multi-factor driven  

---

## 💵 Compensation & Benefits
- Higher income → higher retention  
- Employees with **stock options** stayed significantly longer  
- No stock options → higher attrition  

**Insight:**
Stock ownership strengthens long-term commitment.

---

## 🌍 Demographic Insights
- Single employees left more than married/divorced  
- Male employees had higher attrition  
- Highest attrition: **Under 25 & 25–34 age groups**  
- Bachelor’s/Master’s holders left more than other education groups  

**Interpretation:**
Certain groups may have higher career mobility and external opportunities.

---

## 🏢 Department Analysis
(Adjusted for department size)

- **Sales** → Highest attrition (~21%)  
- **HR** → Moderate (~19%)  
- **R&D** → Lowest (~14%)  

**Insight:**
Sales is the true attrition risk—not R&D (despite higher raw counts).

---

## ⚠️ Key Insights
- Early-career employees are the highest-risk group  
- Salary increases alone do not guarantee retention  
- Engagement and satisfaction influence attrition but are not sole drivers  
- Stock options significantly improve retention  
- Sales department faces the highest attrition risk  

---

## ✅ Recommendations

### 1. Address Sales Attrition
- Conduct targeted exit interviews  
- Improve workload support and coaching  
- Review compensation structures  

### 2. Strengthen Early-Career Retention
- Improve onboarding and mentorship programs  
- Provide clear career progression paths  
- Offer internal mobility opportunities  

### 3. Rethink Compensation Strategy
- Ensure salary increases are timely and proactive  
- Pair raises with career development  

### 4. Improve Employee Engagement
- Conduct regular engagement surveys  
- Improve manager communication  
- Implement recognition programs  

### 5. Expand Stock-Based Incentives
- Introduce or expand stock options  
- Use equity incentives to retain high-performing employees
